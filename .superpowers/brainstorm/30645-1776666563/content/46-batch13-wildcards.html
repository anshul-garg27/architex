W10

ELI5 / ELI-Senior toggle · same concept, different altitude

Slider at top of lesson: Explain Like I'm 5 (warm analogies, no jargon) · Standard (default) · Senior (no hand-holding, technical depth, acronyms expected). Same content, dramatically different voice. Users choose their altitude per lesson.

👶 ELI5
📖 Standard
🧙 ELI-Senior
👶 ELI5: Observer is like a newspaper subscription. You sign up, and the newspaper sends you stuff when new news happens. You don't have to keep asking "is there news yet?"
📖 Standard: Observer is a behavioral pattern where a subject maintains a list of dependents (observers) that are automatically notified of state changes, typically via an update() method.
🧙 ELI-Senior: Observer: intra-process pub-sub with hard-referenced subs → memory-leak vector; use WeakRef or explicit teardown. O(n) fan-out, blocks notifier thread — gate with executor if downstream IO. Degenerates into event bus at scale, then into Kafka. At that point you've rediscovered the whole ecosystem and should admit it.

💡 MY RECOMMENDATION

3. Add Drill as a mode (reuses canvas + adds timer/grader wrapper)

5. Polish what exists — typography rhythm in right pane, status bar, mobile responsiveness

Rebuild would be waste. 2 months of work for something that's already 80% there. Use those 2 months to ship Learn/Drill/Review modes + 90+ ideas from our batches.
