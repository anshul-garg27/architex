Takeaway: code + UML + tradeoffs all exist. Narrative content (hook, analogy, checkpoints) is the gap.

Four ways to fill the content gap

A

Hand-write all 36 before shipping

Writer (you or hired) drafts + polishes all 36 × 8 sections = 288 sections. Quality bar highest. Ship date furthest.

Time: 5-6 weeks full-time
Ship delay: ~6 weeks
Quality: Highest
B

Ship Phase 1 with 10 polished patterns only

Launch Learn mode with 10 creational+structural patterns. Other 26 show "Learn mode coming soon for this pattern" + link to Build mode. Add 5-10 patterns per month.

Time: 1-2 weeks polish
Ship delay: Minimal
Quality: High but limited
C

AI-generate all 36 (Claude Sonnet), human QA

Use your existing Claude integration. Build a prompt pipeline that produces all 8 sections per pattern. Human reviews & edits inconsistencies.

Time: 2-3 weeks
Ship delay: Moderate
Quality: Uneven — AI stumbles on nuance
D
⭐ MY RECOMMENDATION

Hybrid · Top 10 polished + AI drafts for 26 + progressive polish

Ship with 10 hand-polished patterns (Singleton, Factory Method, Builder, Observer, Strategy, Decorator, Adapter, Facade, Template Method, State). Auto-generate drafts for remaining 26 using Claude now; they sit in a "draft" state — readable but marked. Polish on rolling basis; promote to "published" as ready.

Time: 2 weeks to ship, ongoing
Ship delay: Minimal
Quality: Top 10 perfect, rest evolving in public
💡 WHY D IS THE PLAY
A risks months of delay for a 10/10 launch — but Architex is 8-12% complete per your research; velocity matters more than pattern #36 being perfect on day 1.

B makes Architex look incomplete — "Coming soon" badges on 72% of the catalog is weak signal.

C alone is risky — pure AI content on a technical platform can introduce subtle wrong claims (Observer confused with Pub-Sub, Singleton misstated as thread-safe by default). You'd be debugging content bugs for weeks post-launch.

D takes the best of each: high-quality anchor (top 10), full catalog coverage (AI drafts for 26), clear quality signaling (badge: "Draft — help us polish this" inviting community contributions). Each draft → published promotion is a small unit of work trackable via DB flag.
