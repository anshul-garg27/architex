Q3 · When a learner answers a checkpoint wrong, what happens?

Checkpoints are the highest-friction moments in Learn mode. The failure response is where education happens or where users churn. Four schools of thought.

📚 PRIOR-ART COMPARE
Duolingo
Forces correct answer. Lose a "heart". 5 hearts = gated until refill. Pressure cooker.
Brilliant
Shows correct + detailed explanation after any answer. Skip button always present.
Khan Academy
3 wrong → "need a hint?" → keeps nudging. Eventually unlocks "show solution".
LeetCode
Never forces. "Show solution" always there. Up to user.
A

Strict · must-answer-correctly to proceed

User keeps attempting. After each wrong answer: "Not quite — try again." No reveal, no hint. Unlimited attempts but gated progression.

❌ "Not quite. Try again."
❌ "Not quite. Try again."
❌ "Not quite. Try again."
✓ "Correct! Moving on."
Pros: No cheating. True comprehension required.
Cons: Rage-quit magnet. Doesn't teach why wrong. Infantilizing to pros.
B
⭐ MY RECOMMENDATION

Progressive reveal · wrong answers become teachable moments

Each wrong answer gets a targeted explanation (data already exists in quizQuestions.options[].whyWrong). After 3 attempts, reveal correct answer + full explanation. User can always skip voluntarily — no forced gate.

Attempt 1 — wrong
"Not quite. Here's why that's not right: [whyWrong text for that option]. Try again?"
Attempt 2 — wrong
"Still off. Consider: [hint text]. One more shot or [Reveal answer]."
Attempt 3 — wrong or reveal clicked
"The answer is B. Reason: [full explanation]. [Next section →]"
✓ Correct any time
"Correct. [Full explanation so you know why]. [Next →]"
Pros: Failure becomes teaching. Never hostile. whyWrong data already seeded in DB. FSRS gets good signal (1 attempt vs 3 attempts = different retention score).
Cons: Content cost — need quality whyWrong for every option (Claude Opus writes these).
C

Ungated · checkpoints are fully optional

User can skip any checkpoint with a "Skip →" button always visible. Wrong answer = subtle red dot, no drama. Progression not gated.

[Options visible · always] [Skip this →]
Pros: Zero friction. Respects user.
Cons: Most users skip = learn less = completion metrics become a lie. Defeats the point of checkpoints.
D

Adaptive · difficulty self-tunes

After 2 wrong → offer "easier version" of same question. After correct on easy → show harder follow-up. ML-driven calibration over time.

Pros: Personalized. Nobody stuck, nobody bored.
Cons: 3x content authoring (easy/medium/hard variants). Hard to tune. Overkill for v1.
💡 WHY B WINS HERE
Research found quizQuestions.options[].whyWrong data already seeded in DB — 72 of them were filled in the content audit. That infrastructure exists. Option B uses it where it matters most.

Cognitive science also backs it: spaced feedback (attempt → explanation → retry) produces 2-3x better retention than either forced-correct (Duolingo) or reveal-only (Khan). Wrong-answer explanations activate the "productive failure" effect from Manu Kapur's research — one of the 9 research foundations already in your README.
