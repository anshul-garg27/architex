Q15 · When the system detects a stuck learner, what happens?

frustration-detector.ts already exists with 4 levels (calm → mild → frustrated → very-frustrated) and action scoring. Until now, nothing consumes it. What's the intervention policy?

🤖 DETECTOR CAPABILITIES (already built)
Tracks these actions with scores: undo: 2 · redo: 2 · failed-attempt: 4 · hint-used: 3 · pause: 1 · success: -3 · interaction: 0
Rapid-cycle bonus: +5 if 3+ undos within 5s (thrashing). Thresholds: mild: 8 · frustrated: 18 · very-frustrated: 30. Fires callback on level change — ready to hook up.
A

Silent · log only, never intervene

Detector fires, event logs to DB, product team reviews patterns. User sees nothing.

Pros: Zero intervention risk. Clean analytics.
Cons: Wastes existing detector. Frustrated users abandon silently = churn data but no recovery.
B

Gentle · one-shot nudge at mild

At mild level, show a toast: "Stuck? Ask the Architect." No further escalation.

Pros: Low-risk. Simple.
Cons: One toast is too little for very-frustrated. No safety net.
C
⭐ MY RECOMMENDATION

Escalating · 4 levels of increasing help

Detector level → intervention. Each level does more. User can dismiss any level to de-escalate back.

🟦 Calm (score < 8)
No intervention. Detector logs for analytics.
🟩 Mild (8-17)
Gentle inline prompt at bottom of lesson column: "Taking your time on this one. Want a nudge? [Hint]". Dismissable. One-time per section.
🟨 Frustrated (18-29)
Pro-active help offer: floating assistant appears bottom-right: "I see you're working through this. Want me to explain the tricky part? [Yes, explain] [I've got it]". AI opens pre-loaded with current section context.
🟥 Very Frustrated (30+)
Permission-asking pattern. Full-pane but dismissable card: "This pattern's tricky. A few options:"
[Jump to section summary] · [Try an easier pattern first (we'll suggest one)] · [Take a break · come back later] · [Keep going]
No shame. Never blocks. Reverse-recommend path based on prerequisite DAG.
PER-MODE NUANCES
📖 Learn: interventions above · all levels active
🎨 Build: only mild-level nudge appropriate · "Stuck designing? Try a template." Pros shouldn't be interrupted.
⏱️ Drill: NEVER intervene mid-drill — simulating interview means no safety net. Post-submit, if they failed 2+ times and frustration was high, show the very-frustrated card.
🔁 Review: high rating variance only. "Struggling on Observer? [Jump to Learn mode for Observer]"
Pros: Uses existing infrastructure. Matches intensity to need. Drill respects interview realism. Very-frustrated card reduces churn on hard patterns.
Cons: 4-level state machine to get right. Tuning scores matters. ≈ 10h including QA.
D

Full adaptive · auto-everything

System auto-simplifies the lesson, auto-reveals hints, auto-skips difficult sections. "AI decides you're stuck and fixes it."

Pros: Most "intelligent" feeling.
Cons: Removes user agency. Infantilizing. Also — 4-level tuning is hard; 10-level fully-adaptive tuning is impossible v1.
💡 WHY C · ESCALATING AGENCY
Intervention matches intensity. A user at "mild" doesn't need a full break suggestion — they need a small nudge. A user at "very-frustrated" needs options, not another "try again!". The 4 tiers create proportional responses.

Never-intervene-in-Drill is load-bearing. Drill's value prop is interview simulation. Interrupting with "take a break?" during a timed drill undermines the whole point. Post-submit intervention preserves the simulation and still offers recovery.

The very-frustrated card is the anti-churn feature. Users who hit a wall silently rage-quit. Offering explicit options — especially "try an easier pattern first" using the DAG — converts churn into a graceful detour.

No autonomous action. System offers, user decides. Every intervention is dismissable, returnable, never sticks.
