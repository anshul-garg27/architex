Who is the "beginner" we're designing for?

Pick one. This shapes the whole UX — layout, copy, onboarding, content depth.

A

Absolute Newcomer

Knows some programming (Python/JS) but shaky on OOP itself. Has heard "design pattern" but can't define it. Asks: "Why do I even need this?"

Entry experience needs: OOP primer → "what problem does a pattern solve" → first pattern as concrete story

B

OOP-Familiar Learner my guess

Comfortable with classes, inheritance, interfaces. 1-3 years of coding. Never systematically studied patterns. Asks: "Why Strategy vs Observer? What's the real difference?"

Entry experience needs: Real problem hook → pattern reveal → UML → code → when NOT to use

C

Interview-Prep Cruncher

Read Head First / GoF. Knows 20+ patterns by name. Needs drill, retrieval practice, honest grading. Asks: "Test me hard. Where am I weak?"

Entry experience needs: Skip teaching → straight to problems → timer + auto-grade → SRS review queue

D

All of the above — the UI must serve all

Real-world case: any visitor could be A, B, C, or D. Home screen must detect / let them self-select, then branch.

Entry experience needs: Onboarding gate with 3 clear paths: "Teach me" / "Practice" / "Drill & grade"

Why I guessed B: Your product vision doc already targets "Interview Prep Student (60%)" as primary — those are mostly B. You said "jise kuch nahi aata" which leans A, but a pure A-persona on an interview-prep platform is rare. B is also a superset: if we nail B, we can bolt a thin OOP primer on for A, and a fast-track drill mode for C. Safer starting point than trying to serve all 4 at once.
