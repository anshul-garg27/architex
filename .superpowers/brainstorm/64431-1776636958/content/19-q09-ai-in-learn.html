Q9 · Does "Ask the Architect" appear in Learn mode?

Your brand voice doc already names the AI tutor "Ask the Architect" (Matrix reference, avoids tutor hierarchy). The question: in Learn mode, where every section is already curated by Opus, does AI add value or undermine the lesson?

🤔 THE REAL DILEMMA
Learn mode lesson = 1000%-quality content written by Claude Opus 4.7 (Q1 decision). Everything the learner needs is already there. Adding a "Ask AI anything" button could:

✓ Answer specific questions the lesson can't anticipate ("How does this differ in Rust?")
✗ Let learners bypass the struggle productive failure requires
✗ Fragment the pedagogical arc (lesson → AI detour → where was I?)
✓ Cover edge cases in "related patterns" or personal code context
A

No AI in Learn · lesson is the teacher

Learn mode has zero AI surface. Lesson content is authoritative. AI appears only in Build mode (design review), Drill mode (hints), and maybe Review mode (wrong-answer explanations).

Pros: Forces the lesson to be good. No AI crutch. Cheap (no token burn on readers).
Cons: Questions the lesson can't predict go unanswered. Curious learners leave to ChatGPT.
B

Global floating "Ask the Architect" · always available

Persistent purple button bottom-right. Click anywhere → chat panel opens. User can ask anything — about the current pattern, about related patterns, about their own code.

Pros: Flexible. Serves every curiosity.
Cons: Crutch factor — learners ask AI instead of wrestling with the lesson. Token cost per session balloons. Can degrade into "AI did my homework".
C
⭐ MY RECOMMENDATION

Contextual · AI appears at struggle points, not ambient

AI is invoked, not ambient. Three specific surfaces:

① After 3 failed checkpoint attempts
Reveal reveals correct answer + "Want a deeper explanation? [Ask the Architect →]". Opens a focused mini-chat about that specific checkpoint.
② At the end of each lesson section
Small prompt: "Questions about this section? [Ask →]" — pre-seeded with context "you just finished §3 of Factory Method lesson". User types, AI answers with that context.
③ Confused-With section · interactive compare
When lesson shows "Don't confuse with Strategy", a button "Ask me about their difference in your codebase" → opens AI chat with both patterns loaded as context.
Pros: AI appears when learner is ready to receive it (after struggle, at section end, during comparison). Preserves productive failure. Token cost bounded (only fires at 3 specific surfaces). Each invocation has rich context (which section, which pattern, which checkpoint) — Claude's answers are specific, not generic.
Cons: Learners who want to ask a random off-topic question ("How does this work in Go?") can't — they'd need to go to Build mode's "Ask the Architect".
D

AI-generated "deeper dives" inline · no chat UI

No chat interface. Instead, each section has a "Deeper dive" button that triggers a pre-generated AI expansion on that section's concept. Pre-computed, cached, fast.

Pros: No token cost at runtime (pre-gen). No chat complexity.
Cons: Not interactive. Can't answer user's specific question. Just another content layer.
💡 WHY C · INVOKED, NOT AMBIENT
The principle: AI appears where it teaches better than the lesson could — and nowhere else.

After 3 failed attempts: learner has earned personalized explanation. This is the exact moment Manu Kapur's productive failure says retention happens — right after struggle, before apathy.

At section end: concept is fresh, learner might have a follow-up. Context is rich (Claude knows what you just read). This is where generic chat produces generic answers but contextual chat produces brilliant ones.

At "Confused-With": the lesson statically contrasts two patterns, but learner's specific confusion is personal. "In my Payment service should I use Strategy or State?" — only AI can answer that.

Cost discipline: limiting AI to 3 surfaces caps token burn per learner session. Using Haiku for ① (checkpoint explainer, short) and Sonnet for ② ③ (deeper) keeps costs ~$0.03 per lesson even at heavy use.
