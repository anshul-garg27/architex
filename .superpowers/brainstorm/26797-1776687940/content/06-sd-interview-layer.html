The premise · SD interview is its own animal

LLD drills ask "implement these classes". SD interviews ask "design Twitter in 45 minutes, also explain every tradeoff". They have a 5-stage structure candidates routinely bomb. Every FAANG pays $400k+ for this skill. Every existing prep tool (Educative, ByteByteGo, Grokking) ships static content. Ours ships a living canvas + real interviewer + real stakes. This batch designs that layer.

🎯
THE 45-MINUTE PRINCIPLE
"You don't design Twitter in 45 minutes. You perform designing Twitter in 45 minutes — while the interviewer watches you think."
The SD interview is two games at once: the design game (is the architecture correct?) and the performance game (did you clarify? estimate? justify? respond to pushback?). Most candidates obsess over the first and lose on the second. Our interview layer trains both simultaneously — by making the performance game visible, gradable, repeatable.

Q17 · Enforce the 5-stage structure?

Real SD interviews follow a predictable arc. Strong candidates move through it deliberately. Weak ones skip straight to boxes-and-arrows. Do we enforce the stages in UI?

OPTION A · FREE-FORM
User gets canvas + problem statement. Draws whatever they want. No stage enforcement. Grading based on what they produced by timer-end.
Pros: matches real interview freedom. Cons: beginners skip clarification, fail fast.
⭐ OPTION B · GATED STAGES
Five explicit stages. User can't advance without a minimum commitment. Canvas unlocks only after clarifying. Deep-dive unlocks only after high-level design.
① CLARIFY · 5 min · ask 3 questions, get constraints
② ESTIMATE · 5 min · Fermi math on QPS/storage
③ HIGH-LEVEL DESIGN · 15 min · canvas, main components
④ DEEP DIVE · 15 min · interviewer picks 1-2 components
⑤ TRADE-OFFS & FOLLOW-UP · 5 min · Q&A, alternatives
Pros: teaches the arc. Cons: feels on-rails at first.
OPTION C · STAGES BUT ADVISORY
Stages shown in top bar with elapsed time. User can move freely but gets "you're 12 min in and haven't clarified anything — interviewers note this" nudges. Penalty at grade time, not locked in UI.
Pros: flexibility + awareness. Cons: some users ignore.
OPTION D · PERSONA-SCALED
Rookie: gated (B). Journeyman: advisory (C). Architect: free-form (A). Matches skill level. Requires more UX work but honors learner.

Q18 · Interview modes to ship (multi-select)

Different users want different stakes. Not every session is a high-pressure timed mock.

① STUDY MODE ⭐
must-ship
No timer. Unlimited hints. User learns the problem shape. Canonical solution revealable at any time. For first-time exposure to a problem.
② TIMED MOCK ⭐
must-ship
45- or 60-minute timer. Pauses permitted (with warning). Hints available but cost rubric points. Simulates the real thing.
③ EXAM MODE ⭐
the stress-test
45 min. No pauses. No hints. No canvas templates. Graded end-of-session. Tracks cumulative streak. The "can I do this when it matters?" mode.
④ PAIR MODE ⭐
our moat
AI (Sonnet) interviews live. Asks clarifying Qs. Pushes on weak spots. Drops hints when stuck. Real back-and-forth via text OR voice. Nothing else in market does this at this depth.
⑤ REVIEW MODE
reuse past
Replay any past attempt with annotations. See your reasoning. Compare to canonical at each stage. Rewinds are free; no penalty.
⑥ FULL-STACK LOOP
cross-module
90-min double session. SD round (45) then LLD round (45). Mirrors actual FAANG onsite. Same problem in two lenses ("design Uber at scale → now implement dispatch algo"). Deep cross-module integration.
⑦ VERBAL MODE
hard-mode
Mic on. User narrates design aloud while drawing. AI transcribes + grades verbal explanation quality. Simulates whiteboard-talking-to-human-at-Google.

Q19 · AI interviewer personas (multi-select)

One AI, many voices. Different personas drill different muscles.

① THE STAFF ENGINEER ⭐
Senior+ at big tech. Asks for tradeoffs on everything. "Why CDN here?" "Why not this instead?" Warm but rigorous. Default persona.
② THE BAR-RAISER ⭐
Amazon-style. Probes for weakness. "What happens if your cache is cold?" "You said 10k rps — defend that number." Intentionally adversarial but fair.
③ THE COACH
Bootcamp instructor vibe. Drops gentle hints when stuck. "Have you thought about caching?" Scores less harshly. For warmup sessions.
④ THE SKEPTIC
Meta-engineer style. "Prove it'll work." "What's your p99?" "Why this and not Kafka?" Forces quantification at every step.
⑤ THE PRINCIPAL
"So we did this in 2018 and it didn't work because..." Drops real-world war stories mid-interview. Tests if candidate can absorb prior context.
⑥ THE INDUSTRY SPECIALIST
Switches persona by problem domain. Fintech-interviewer for payments. Gaming-interviewer for matchmaking. Context-appropriate lens.
⑦ THE COMPANY PRESET
Named presets: "Google Bar-Raiser", "Meta L5 Loop", "Amazon Leadership Principles", "Stripe Senior Eng". Each persona rubric-tuned to real feedback.
⑧ THE SILENT WATCHER
Barely speaks. Occasional "hmm" or "go on". Simulates interviewers who want you to drive. Hardest mode — no external structure.

Q21 · Post-interview artifacts — what gets generated?

End-of-session deliverables. Multi-select.

① RUBRIC BREAKDOWN ⭐
6-axis score: Clarification · Estimation · HL Design · Deep-Dive · Tradeoffs · Communication. Each axis 1-5. With 1-sentence rationale. Chartable over time.
② AI POST-MORTEM ⭐
Sonnet-generated narrative: "you spent 22 min on clarification (too much). Your estimate was off by 10x. Strong move: introducing the cache mid-design. Weak moment: didn't justify single DB."
③ CANONICAL COMPARE ⭐
Side-by-side: your design vs. the canonical (from Batch 3's templates). Diff-highlighted. "Canonical uses Redis streams here — you used RabbitMQ."
④ TIMING HEATMAP
Timeline of 45 min. Where did you spend time? Compared to optimal split. Reveals time-management patterns.
⑤ FOLLOW-UP DRILLS
"You struggled on estimation — try these 3 warmup problems." Personalized next-step queue.
⑥ SIMULATE YOUR DESIGN
One-click button: "run chaos drill on your design". Link to Simulate mode (Batch 4) with user's interview design pre-loaded.
⑦ SHAREABLE RECAP
Export as PDF (or link). Rubric + design + post-mortem. User can share with mentor, coach, or recruiter. Subtle marketing.
⑧ STREAK & STATS
Profile dashboard: # interviews done, avg rubric score, strongest/weakest axis, domain coverage. Gamifies consistency.

Q22 · SD-LLD crossover in Drill mode

Users preparing for onsite loops face BOTH SD and LLD rounds. How much do we integrate?

OPTION A · SIBLING MODULES
Keep LLD and SD drills separate. Same UI shell, independent progress. User picks which to practice.
⭐ OPTION B · LINKED + OPTIONAL LOOP
Separate drills PLUS a "Full-Stack Loop" option: 45 min SD → 10 min break → 45 min LLD on a related problem. Same canonical problem domain. User unlocks after doing 5 each.
Example loop:
SD round: "Design Uber at scale · matching + routing + payment"
LLD round: "Implement the dispatch matching algo from your SD"
OPTION C · DEEP INTEGRATION
A SD design can "zoom in" on any component → triggers an LLD drill for that component. A LLD pattern can "zoom out" → triggers an SD design for "where is this pattern used at scale?". Knowledge graph wire.
OPTION D · ALL THREE
Sibling drills (A) + full-stack loops (B) + zoom-in/out integration (C). Everything wired. Maximum moat. Incremental ship.
