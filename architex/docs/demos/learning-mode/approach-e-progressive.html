The Problem
Imagine you subscribe to a YouTube channel. You don't check every hour for new videos — you get notified. That's the Observer pattern: let interested parties know when something changes, instead of having them poll.
Meet the Subject
The Subject keeps a list of everyone who cares about its state. It can attach(), detach(), and notify() observers.

The Contract
The Observer interface defines the contract: any class that implements update() can subscribe to the Subject.

This decoupling is the pattern's superpower — the Subject doesn't need to know what its observers are, only that they implement update().
